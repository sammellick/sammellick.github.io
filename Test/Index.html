About Me

Hello! I am an Adiunkt (Assistant Professor) at Jagiellonian University in Kraków, Poland, where I am a member of the group of Mikolaj Fraczyk. My academic journey has taken me from a PhD at Central European University to postdoctoral positions at McGill University and École Normale Supérieure de Lyon. My research interests are primarily in group theory and its connections to geometry and dynamics.

I believe mathematics is not just about solving problems, but about discovering new ways of thinking and seeing the world. As a "visionary" and "dreamweaver," I am always seeking to connect disparate ideas and bring new mathematical landscapes to life. My background as an "actor" helps me communicate complex ideas in an engaging and accessible way, both in the classroom and at conferences.
